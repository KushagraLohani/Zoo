TAUCAN

Scientific Name: Ramphastos toco

Habitat: Found in tropical and subtropical rainforests of Central and South America.

Appearance: Known for their large, colorful beaks and black body with white throat patches.

Diet: Omnivores that eat fruits, insects, small reptiles, and bird eggs.

Behavior: Social birds that live in small flocks and communicate with croaking or clicking sounds.

Beak Use: The large beak helps reach fruits and also regulates body temperature.

Flight: Short-distance fliers that hop between trees more than they fly.

Conservation Status: Some species are threatened due to deforestation and habitat loss.

Symbolism: Represent playfulness, color, and tropical beauty.
