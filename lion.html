LION

Scientific Name: Panthera leo

Habitat: Found mainly in Africa, with a small population in India (Gir Forest).

Appearance: Lions have muscular bodies, short coats, and a tufted tail. Males have a distinctive mane.

Social Structure: Lions are the only cats that live in groups called prides.

Diet: They are carnivores, mainly hunting zebras, antelopes, and buffaloes.

Role in the Ecosystem: Known as apex predators, they help control prey populations.

Communication: Lions communicate through roars, scent marking, and body language.

Roar: A lion’s roar can be heard up to 8 kilometers (5 miles) away.

Lifespan: Around 10–14 years in the wild, and up to 20 years in captivity.

Symbolism: Lions often symbolize strength, courage, and leadership.
