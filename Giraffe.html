GIRAFFE

Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis

Habitat: Found in savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands of Africa.

Appearance: The giraffe is the tallest land animal, with a long neck, long legs, and spotted coat patterns.

Diet: Herbivores that feed mainly on leaves, flowers, and fruits from tall trees like acacias.

Behavior: Giraffes are gentle and social animals that live in loose herds.

Neck Use: The long neck helps them reach high branches and is also used by males in combat.

Height: Adult giraffes can grow up to 5–6 meters tall.

Lifespan: Live around 20–25 years in the wild.

Adaptations: Their long tongues and strong hearts help them survive in dry habitats.

Symbolism: Giraffes symbolize vision, grace, and uniqueness.
