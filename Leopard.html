LEOPARD

Scientific Name: Panthera pardus

Habitat: Found in forests, grasslands, mountains, and even deserts.

Appearance: Has golden-yellow fur with black spots called rosettes.

Diet: A carnivore, eats deer, monkeys, and smaller animals.

Behavior: Leopards are solitary and mostly nocturnal hunters.

Climbing Skill: Excellent climbers — often drag prey up trees to eat safely.

Speed: Can run up to 58 km/h (36 mph).

Adaptability: Can survive in many different environments.

Lifespan: Lives around 12–17 years in the wild.

Conservation Status: Vulnerable due to habitat loss and poaching.
