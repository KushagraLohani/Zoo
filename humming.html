HUMMING BIRD

Scientific Name: Trochilidae

Habitat: Found mainly in the Americas, especially in tropical and subtropical regions.

Appearance: Small birds with iridescent feathers, long slender bills, and tiny bodies.

Diet: Feed primarily on nectar from flowers, but also eat small insects and spiders.

Behavior: Hummingbirds are known for their rapid wing flapping and ability to hover in mid-air.

Flight Skill: Can fly forwards, backwards, and even upside down for short periods.

Size: Among the smallest birds in the world, some species weigh less than 2 grams.
