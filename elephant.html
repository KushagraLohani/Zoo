ELEPHANT

Scientific Name: Elephas maximus (Asian Elephant) / Loxodonta africana (African Elephant)

Habitat: Found in forests, grasslands, and savannas across Africa and Asia.

Appearance: Elephants are the largest land animals with thick gray skin, large ears, and a long trunk.

Diet: Herbivores that eat grass, leaves, fruits, and bark.

Behavior: Elephants are social animals that live in groups called herds, led by a matriarch.

Trunk Use: The trunk is used for breathing, eating, drinking, and communication.

Intelligence: Known for their high intelligence, strong memory, and emotional sensitivity.

Conservation Status: Many elephant species are endangered due to poaching and habitat loss.

Symbolism: Elephants symbolize wisdom, strength, and loyalty.
