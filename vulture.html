VULTURE

Scientific Name: Accipitridae (Old World Vultures) / Cathartidae (New World Vultures)

Habitat: Found in open areas, grasslands, and savannas across every continent except Australia and Antarctica.

Appearance: Large birds with bald heads, broad wings, and strong hooked beaks.

Diet: Scavengers that feed mainly on the carcasses of dead animals.

Behavior: Play an important role in cleaning the environment by consuming decaying flesh.

Flight Skill: Excellent fliers that soar at high altitudes using air currents to save energy.

Vision: Have extremely sharp eyesight to spot food from long distances.

Conservation Status: Many species are endangered due to poisoning and habitat loss.

Symbolism: Represent renewal, purification, and the balance of nature.
