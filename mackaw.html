MACAW

Scientific Name: Ara spp.

Habitat: Found in tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

Appearance: Large, colorful parrots with long tails and strong curved beaks.

Diet: Eat fruits, nuts, seeds, and occasionally insects.

Behavior: Highly social birds that live in flocks and are very vocal.

Flight Skill: Strong fliers capable of covering long distances in the wild.

Lifespan: Can live 50 years or more in captivity, slightly less in the wild.

Intelligence: Known for problem-solving skills and ability to mimic sounds and human speech.

Conservation Status: Many species are endangered due to habitat loss and illegal pet trade.

Symbolism: Represent beauty, intelligence, and communication.
