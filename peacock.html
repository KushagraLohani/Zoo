PEACOCK

Scientific Name: Pavo cristatus

Habitat: Found mainly in forests, farmlands, and open areas of India and Sri Lanka.

Appearance: Males have vibrant iridescent blue and green feathers with long tail plumes, females are duller in color.

Diet: Omnivores that eat seeds, insects, small reptiles, and plants.

Behavior: Known for courtship displays where males fan out their tail feathers to attract females.

Flight: Can fly short distances despite their heavy tail feathers.

Sound: Make loud calls, especially during mating season.

Conservation Status: Least Concern, but populations are affected by habitat loss.

Symbolism: Represents beauty, grace, pride, and immortality.
